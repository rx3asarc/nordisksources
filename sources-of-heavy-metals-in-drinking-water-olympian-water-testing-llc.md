## What are Heavy Metals?
The heavy metals are those elements with an atomic weight and density higher than that of most other elements.  Metals are present naturally, but they can also be discharged into the environment through human activities like mining, manufacturing and dumping.  Some heavy metals like iron, copper and zinc are necessary for good health when used in small quantities, but can be dangerous when consumed in large quantities.

### Health Risks Associated with Heavy Metal Exposure
Even the very smallest amounts of heavy metals can have serious health effects, such as: 
 Neurological Effects 
 Heavy metals like lead and mercury can have profound neurological effects including brain and nervous system damage.  Children, especially, are prone to lead poisoning, which leads to delays and learning disabilities. 
 Cardiovascular Effects 
 Heavy metals like lead and cadmium also have cardiovascular effects like increased risk of heart disease and stroke. 
 Reproductive Effects 
 Heavy metals like lead and mercury can also be toxic in a reproductive sense causing infertility and birth defects. 
 Cancer 
 Excessive levels of certain heavy metals — including cadmium and arsenic — are also linked to certain types of cancer, such as lung, bladder and kidney.

### [Read More »](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Folympianwatertesting.com%2Fglycols-in-drinking-water-sources-and-occurrence%2F)Protecting Yourself and Your Family from Heavy Metals in Drinking Water
You can do the following to help keep your family and you safe from heavy metals in water: 
 Check Your Water: You can test your water to find out if your water is heavy metal contaminated.  You can get your water tested by a registered lab or you can purchase a test kit at home.  Checking your water regularly can make sure that it is safe and doesn’t contain dangerous contaminants. 
 Install a Water Filter: An activated carbon or reverse osmosis water filter will draw heavy metals out of your water.  When shopping for a water filter, make sure to choose one that is certified to remove the heavy metals in question for your location.  That will make sure that your water is clean and free of bad pollutants. 
 Cooking and Drinking With Cold Water: Hot water is better for evaporating more heavy metals from the pipes and fixtures so always cook and drink with cold water.  If your home has lead pipes or appliances, run the water for a few minutes before using it so you don’t expose yourself to lead.  In one simple act, you will reduce your intake of heavy metals in water. 
 Replace Old Infrastructure: If your home has old infrastructure like lead pipes or plumbing fixtures, replace them so you don’t need to be exposed to heavy metals.  It’s a big investment, but it is for the health of you and your family.  Replacement of old infrastructure can ensure that your water is not contaminated with harmful chemicals. 
 Be Affirmative of Safe and Sustainable Water Uses: Adopting safe and sustainable water use practices (eg, good waste disposal, environmentally sound industrial practices) can decrease heavy metals being discharged into the atmosphere and save our drinking water.  You can help make sure that the future generation will have safe and clean water by advocating for sustainable water practices.
### The Importance of Monitoring Heavy Metals in Drinking Water
It is also necessary to keep an eye on the heavy metals in water so they don’t exceed safe levels.  Heavy metals have maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) in water from the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), and water utilities are required to track the contaminants in their water supply on a regular basis.  Water utilities have a duty to reduce heavy metal concentrations in the water so that the water meets the MCLs.
### What are the roles of water utilities in protecting drinking water quality?
Water companies are the key to providing safe, quality water.  They are the ones to check for levels of heavy metals in the drinking water, and make adjustments to remove these from the water.  Also, water companies maintain and modernize their infrastructure to make sure that water is clean from pollutants such as heavy metals.  Water utilities can also work in concert with regulators and consumers to ensure that everyone’s drinking water is safe and clean.
### The Benefits of Drinking Heavy Metal-Free Water
We all need water that is not tainted with heavy metals for good health, and there are numerous advantages to using water that is clean of these chemicals.  Consuming water that is free of heavy metals will lessen your risk for serious diseases, such as brain damage, heart disease, fertility issues, and cancer.  Furthermore,[heavy metal-free water](https://www.google.com/url?sa=E&q=https%3A%2F%2Folympianwatertesting.com%2Fhow-to-test-and-treat-heavy-metals-in-drinking-water-a-practical-guide%2F)is better for health and wellbeing, as well as a sense of security that the water you and your family are drinking is not contaminated by any toxic contaminants.

Through the collection of your water quality for heavy metals, communication with water providers for safe, clean water, and taking action to mitigate exposure to these contaminants, you can make sure your drinking water is safe and free of harmful contaminants.  You should never go without drinking water and you need to make sure that the water you consume is clean and free of toxins such as heavy metals.

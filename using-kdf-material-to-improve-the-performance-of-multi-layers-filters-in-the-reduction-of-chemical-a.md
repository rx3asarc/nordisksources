## Abstract
### Multi-layers filters are usually used in conventional water treatment plants to purify
### water, reduce turbidity, and remove plankton and undesired odours. Multi-layers filter
### consists of basic layers represented in graded sand and supporting gravel layers. This
### research aims to study the efficiency of such filters after the addition of new layers made
### of copper and zinc particles by 50% per each with high purity, which are traditionally
### called KDF (Kinetic Degradation Fluxion). The purpose of adding such layers is to
### increase the efficiency of filters in the removal of diverse chemical contaminants of
### heavy elements (Lead, Nickel, iron, etc.), and chlorine to use them in traditional or
### advanced filter stations, such reverse osmosis plants or ion exchange water filters. A glass
### tube was filled with KDF, sand and gravel layers and used as a multi-layer's filter, and
### tests were carried out on a water sample that has been prepared in the lab with
### concentration of 2 mg/l for heavy elements and 5 mg/l for chlorine. Flow rates of the
### tested water sample were 20, 40, 60, 80 and 100 l/hr. The results show a very high
### efficiency of such filters to remove of chlorine from the treated water by 90% and more,
### while the efficiency of the modified filter to remove lead, nickel and iron was 92%, 88%,
### and 67% respectively; however, there was no significant records in removing the
### biological contaminants.
## Keywords
### Multi-layers filters; KDF; Chlorine; Heavy metals; Water treatment
1. Introduction
### Multilayer filters are used in conventional water treatment plants to treat the small
### suspended particles and reduce the turbidity for drinking water or industrial purposes.
### Most of the disinfection processes are carried out on treated water when it used for
### drink, and they include physical and chemical methods to kill Bacteria and other
### pathogenic microbiology, which cause many diseases to human. Such processes are
### associated with side effect products called as (Disinfection By-products) as a result of
### reaction of chlorine with organic materials which exist in water sources and one of these
### materials is Tri-Halo Methane's THMs which considered as carcinogenic material (Tian et
### al., 2017). Chlorine in all its kinds is considered the main chemical disinfectant, which
### have been used in sterilizing water in 1908, and since that date chlorine was used as an
### Penicillin and other antibiotics in saving the lives of people (Laubusch E, 1971).
### However, the use of chlorine showed side effects that resulted from the reaction of
### chlorine with the existing organic components in water Total Organics Carbons TOC
### (Hubrig and Innovation Services Inc, 2015). Researchers continued to provide disinfection
### methods and materials, which has less or no side effects to human and environment. And
### one of these methods is using new techniques and new materials to increase the
### efficiency of multi-layers’ filters' performance by reducing the side effects on hand and
### reducing costs on other hands (Xiony, 2004). Multi-layer filters differ in their
### components and design depending on the main purpose of using them in filtration
### plants. The most used material as filtering medium are sand which is used in special
### granular gradients and gravel as a supporting layer to the sand, which used to increase
### the efficiency of multiple filters. Other filtration mediums are added such as anthracite
### or activated carbon in its two types (granules and powder) to clear water from odours
### and undesirable gases and reduce dissolved organic compounds to a minimum
### acceptable amount. A thin layer of activated carbon (sometimes fine carbon powder)
### could be added above the fine sand layer during the treatment process called absorptive
### treatment, in which gases and organic materials are absorbed. Despite of the high
### efficiency of activated carbon in reducing and removing the soluble organic materials
### (Total Organic Carbon), the cost of re-activate and use it may not be useful especially
### when materials' concentration is more than 40% (Tobiason et al., 2011). The addition of
### extra filtration medium to multi-layer filters is based on the main purpose of filtration. A
### number of attempts were made to develop the performance of filters, and addition to the
### filtration process, some filters are designed to do other functions when chemical or
### biological mediums are added for the purposes of disinfection and removal of organic
### compounds (Zeppenfeld, 2010). The researcher Labso noticed during his study, the effect
### of some metals on water properties when they are used to disinfect water. He found that
### when water passes on the surface of silver, the taste and odour of water are acceptably
### changed as a result of the effect of silver ion oxides on water properties and
### microbiology. That clarify the use of silver tubes or silver coated tubes to save water
### through old trips of commercial ship (Shuval et al., 1995). In 2004, NASA has introduced
### an innovative system for water purification and disinfection using electrolytic ionization
### of silver used in the Apollo spacecraft (Sanchez et al., 2018). The performance of metals
### or chemical active compounds was not limited by sterilizing only, but also the mineral
### compounds were found to have the ability on reducing contaminants in water such as
### heavy elements and other dissolved ions. They can achieve such goal by mixing certain
### ratios of minerals' grains (with a high degree of purity) to form an alloy that causes an
### ion exchange reaction (oxidation and reduction reactions) in aqueous solutions, in
### addition to the ability of such metal combination on oxidizing micro-organisms. The
### combination of Copper and Zinc powder (with a high degree of purity). He explained
### that free energy of the surface has a significant effect on the movement and transmission
### of ions in aqueous solutions, where these solutions provide electrolyte medium that
### helps activating the ions exchanging process. This technique has been used in reducing
### sediments in stream boilers, and the combination of such mineral compounds was
### laterally renamed as kinetic degradation fluxion (KDF) (Barakat, 2011). The Environment
### Protection Agency EPA has considered KDF as one of the materials, which are used in
### elimination of biogenic pollutants (algae, fungi and bacteria), in addition to its
### effectiveness in reducing heavy elements through oxidation and reduction processes.
### This material was also investigated to have the ability in removing mercury from water
### wells or other water sources by depending a specific combination of Zinc and Copper
### (AGENCY, 2009). Kinetic Degradation Fluxion (KDF) is a high-purity copper-zinc
### formulation that uses a basic chemical process known as redox (oxidation/reduction) to
### remove chlorine, lead, mercury, iron, and hydrogen sulfide from water supplies. The
### process also has a mild anti-bacterial, algaecidic, and fungicidic effect and may reduce
### the accumulation of lime scale (Spiegel et al., 2018). The KDF is environmentally friendly
### material; it can be washed by reverse washing way to reactivate and reuse again, and it
### has an operating life longer than other mediums in the multi-layers’ filters (Jeakle). The
### efficiency of such material is affected by the design factors of filters such as flow rate and
### surface area of the used material and its layer thickness, Also, the pH value has an impact
### on the efficiency and performance of this material (Tijing et al., 2010). Grains size for the
### combination of KDF material and the distribution method has a significant effect on its
### performance and efficiency and the success of the backwash during the process of
### reactivating multi-media filter, and it appeared through experiments that the addition of
### an appropriate layer of metal medium does not affect the pressure drop rates during the
### work of the multi-filter if used within standard operating flow rates for filtration (T. F. L.
### B, 2003).
### The KDF redox process works by exchange electrons with contaminants. This give and
### take of electrons converts may contaminants into harmless components during the
### reaction, electrons are transferred between molecules, and new elements are created.
### Some harmful contaminants are changed into harmless components. Free chlorine KDF
### filters are used most often in conjunction with an activated carbon filter. Similarly, some
### heavy metals such as copper, lead, mercury and others, react to plate out onto the
### medium's surface, thus being effectively removed from the water supply (Roy and
### Hydroviv, 2018).
### This study aims to improve the quality of filters and reduce environmental pollutants to
### protect human health and save the environment, hence; a layer of a chemically active
### and it helps increasing filters’ effectiveness in the elimination of soluble pollutants and
### heavy elements such as lead, nickel and iron. In addition, the study aims to determine
### the ability of this material in minimization of residual free chlorine concentration and
### some biological organisms in water.

## 2.1. Materials
### KDF material supplied by a US company; which is composed of a mixture of high purity
### grains of copper and zinc, 50% per each. The volumetric gradient of the mixture grains
### ranges from 0.146 to 2.0 mm. This material causes chemical reactions when it used in
### aqueous solutions such as Redox (Reduction and Oxidation), which are activated during
### the passing of water on this material, leading to a change in the properties of dissolved
### matters in the water. Oxidation of some material could happen while the reduction of
### other materials is in progress, thus dissolved pollutants are turned into insoluble
### sediments, which can be easily separated and disposed by reverse washing. In the case of
### principle other dissolved elements gets oxidized and turned into insoluble elements in
### the water, such as lead, iron and nickel. Fig. 1 bellows shows the general shape of the KDF
### used in the rest of research and Table 1 shows X-ray analysis of KDF which have been

## 2.2.1. Method
### Several experiments were carried out on water after the addition of specific
### contaminants. Some water samples were containing chlorine with a concentration
### 5 mg/s, while others were prepared with 2 mg/l of heavy elements (lead, iron and nickel).
### The flow rate was determined at a range of 20–80 L/hour, and water were flowing down
### by gravity to a sand filter from a tank placed at the top of the filter tube. KDF layer was
### added with a thickness 50 mm and placed above a 140-mm sand layer which was
### samples were examined by the atomic spectrometer to measure the concentrations of
### heavy elements. While the free chlorine was measured by the chlorine test kite, besides
### other specifications of synthetic water, such as the pH, turbidity, and concentration of
### dissolved salts and water hardness, were also examined.
### After each experiment the medium's layers were replaced to prevent the interacting of
### the previous experiments' contaminants with the new sampled water, and to ensure that
### they are not affecting the results of another metal removing tests. Then water was
### pumped into the filter with different operational flow rates (80, 60, 40, 20 l/hr);
### thereafter, chemical and physics tests were made after each experiment to study the
### effect of the new metal layer on performance of a filter towards contaminates reduction.
3. Results and discussion
### Experiments were carried out on water samples with the specifications shown in Table 2,
### which were prepared in a laboratory using a new multi-media filter. The results of the
### treated samples which contain 5.0 mg/l of chlorine were measured and presented in Fig.
2. Fig. 2 shows a high efficiency of the multi-layer filter in removing chlorine from the
### water; it reveals that the flow rate has a significant impact on chlorine reduction ratio in
### the treated water since the time of detention of water in the filter is inversely
### proportional to flow rate as contact time decreases by increasing the flow rate. Hence,
### the ion exchanging rate between chlorine and the KDF layer is dropping at a short
### detention time due to incomplete reaction. However, the slow flow rates are not always
### the optimal or most efficient in design since the process of reaction activation requires
### an appropriate amount of water solution to ensure the ion exchanging. It can be
### concluded from results that the highest rate of chlorine removal occurred when using a
### 40 L/hour flow rate.

4. Conclusions
### The results indicate that the KDF can be used as an additional layer to improve the
### performance of multi-layered filters, as it is useful to reduce design costs of processing
### units, particularly in the treatment of industrial water. It can be also used in reverse
### osmosis water treatment plants since they have high efficiency in chlorine reduction.
### They are useful in ion exchanges for their efficiency in reducing and removing certain
### heavy elements and chlorine, also can reduce the cost of maintenance and increase the
### operational age of the ion exchanger. Additionally, the use of KDF layer in traditional
### filters helps to reduce the cost and extend the operational life of the filters’ media
### especially to the activated carbon layer, and is an environmentally friendly material.

## Abstract
### Heavy metal pollution in water sources has become a major worldwide environmental
### issue, posing a threat to aquatic ecosystems and human health. The pollution of the
### aquatic environment is increasing as a result of industrialization, climate change, and
### urban development. The sources of heavy metal pollution in water include mining waste,
### leachates from landfills, municipal and industrial wastewater, urban runoff, and natural
### events such as volcanism, weathering, and rock abrasion. Heavy metal ions are toxic and
### potentially carcinogenic. They can also buildup in biological systems and cause

### such as the nervous system, liver and lungs, kidneys and stomach, skin, and reproductive
### systems. There were various approaches tried to purify water and maintain water quality.

## Introduction
### Water bodies are a needed asset for the occurrence of livelihood and the obtainability of
### fresh water is a basic need for people as well as the environment where we are living. In
### any case, over the past years, the essence of water has been badly affected by
### consistently intensifying inhabitants, quick mechanization, escalating urban sprawl, and
### uncaring usage of regular possessions (Carolin et al., 2017; Joseph et al., 2019; Qiao et al.,
### 2020; Vardhan et al., 2019; Sharma et al., 2023). Heavy Metal (HM) is regarded as a
### paramount pollutant in habitats of freshwater (e.g. Lakes, rivers, ponds, and channels),
### because of their tenacity, lethality aggregation, and ability of bio-magnification in the
### edibles cycles (Saher and Siddiqui, 2019; Wang et al., 2019).
### Nearly 71% surface of the world is covered with aquatic bodies while 96.5% of the entire
### world's water is available as seas and 1% of the absolute global water is valuable to help
### the livelihood of people. The value of water is as significant as for environment
### concerning people. Globally, up to 90% of the effluent originated is released directly into
### the environment, resulting in the production of boundless water body contamination.
### The marine environment faces serious issues of heavy metal enrichment and also it plays
### an essential part in causing the major issues in the aquatic territory. Metals present in
### the water bodies are separated between various marine ecological sections and in
### different forms such as softened granular matter, or compound structure (Green and
### Planchart, 2018).
### The harmfulness of the heavy metal leans primarily on the channel of discharge, an
### element of their physical-synthetic resources and ecological condition (Yang et al., 2019).
### Even supposing HM takes no exact description, the analysis states that existing
### components possess high atomic weight and several times density higher than that of
### water bodies (Bánfalvi, 2011). Heavy metal pollution affecting the environment is
### escalating almost all over the place with the development of industrial activities.
### Enriched industrial action afterward chemicals have been ejected due to the industrial
### operations is unique of the reasons for environmental and municipal health troubles. The
### HM occupancy in water resources depends on the effect of anthropogenic actions. In
### poor disposal of waste, vehicle emanate, fossil fuel burning, pesticide, fertilizer
### implementation, and untreated irrigation wastewater (Elnazer et al., 2018; Seleem et al.,
### 2021; Karki et al., 2024). HM at elevated concentrations is precarious for both aquatic
### habitat and human health. Although at normal concentrations the HM is essential when
### it surpasses the concentration thresholds, these HM affects human health (Maret and
### Sandstead, 2006). Thus the effects due to heavy metal contamination are distinguished
### and designated by several authors (Xu et al., 2017, 2020; Yunus et al., 2020). However,
### some HM especially humanity depends on Cu, Fe, and Zn, and other important elements
### include cobalt, lead, chromium, mercury, uranium, selenium, arsenic, cadmium, silver,
### gold, nickel, etc. HM are non-biodegradable and hence considered as a serious hazard to
### aquatic systems and a major source of environmental concern. When HM are introduced
### into an aquatic system, they can interfere with typical aquatic activity, and most HM
### offers substantial health risks to people who drink or recreate within water (Amadi et al.,
### 2017; Azimi et al., 2017; Vilların and Merel, 2020; Zhou et al., 2020). Due to the cause of
### leaching from garbage dumping, degraded environment, and aquatic bodies, metals are
### gathered and carried out into the food chain. An occurrence known as biomagnification
### is when metal concentrations rise at each extent of the food chain and thus proceed on
### to the next elevated level. Metal ions are harmful because of their capacity to attach to
### protein molecules, preventing DNA replication and cell division. The central nervous
### system gets infected due to immoderate intake of lead and cadmium exposure resulting
### in the amplified risk of osteoporosis and lung disorder (cancer) (Vareda et al., 2019).
### Exposure to arsenic creates unfavourable health effects including diabetes, pulmonary
### disease, and cardiovascular disease in China (province of Taiwan) arsenic exposure is
### responsible for the “Blackfoot disease”, a grave illness of blood vessels leading to
### gangrene (Organization, 2004). The exposure of mercury in lesser concentrations
### remains controversial from a clinical aspect. Furthermore, the mercury's deadliness
### varies depending on the pattern, dosage, and exposure rate. The lining in the gut and
### kidneys primarily gets damaged due to exposure to mercuric salts (Joseph et al., 2019;
### Vareda et al., 2019). Zinc exposure is accountable for infertility and kidney disease,
### copper exposure results in depression, and chromium exposure leads to tumors in
### respiratory organs (Pratush et al., 2018; Tan, 2018). Cobalt is rarely detected in aquatic
### systems namely rivers, lakes, and groundwater, (Punsar et al., 1975) due to the presence
### of lesser concentration (0.1–5 μg/L) (Lauwerys and Lison, 1994; Leyssens et al., 2017). In
### the workplace, Co levels are frequently measured at the primary organs namely the skin
### and respiratory system of mankind. The human race subjected to nickel-polluted
### surroundings leads to several health problems (Valko et al., 2007; Zambelli and Ciurli,
### 2013; Zhao et al., 2009), such as adverse effects in human health for instance respiratory
### system tumors, renal, cardiovascular, and lung fibrosis conditions (Malik et al., 2019;
### several severe environmental protection rules and forced the scholarly and business
### communities to work together to clean up the effluents. Due to its non-biodegradable
### characteristic when compared to other organic contaminants, the handling of
### wastewater containing toxic metal requires special thought among all effluents.
### The novelty of this work is related to the focus on water pollution caused by heavy
### metals, as well as different treatment options for heavy metal removal. Additionally, the
### present article examines the various characteristics of toxic heavy metals as hazardous
### contaminants, accompanied by a particular pivotal point on their existence,
### identification, and risk evaluation in aquatic environments. Moreover, this review seeks
### to contribute to the development of sustainable and efficient solutions to reduce the
### environmental impact of heavy metals and to address the challenges and limitations of
### conventional and nonconventional treatment of heavy metal removal strategies. By
### integrating state-of the –art health risk assessment technologies, it provides a roadmap
### to a holistic understanding and sustainable solution to this ubiquitous environmental
### threat, providing new discoveries and integrated solutions to protect our aquatic
### ecosystems and communities that depend on them. Finally, the review provides an
### overview of future perspectives and research directions in this area of heavy metal
### removal from aquatic environments.

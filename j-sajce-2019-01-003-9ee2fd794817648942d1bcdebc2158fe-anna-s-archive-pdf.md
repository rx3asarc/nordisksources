Using KDF material to improve the performance of multi-layers filters in the reduction of chemical and biological pollutants in surface water treatment
Hasan Shaker Majdia,∗, Mahdi Shanshal Jaafarb, Azher M. Abeda
a Al-Mustaqbal University College, Babylon, Iraq bMinistry of Science and Technology, Iraq
A R T I C L E I N F O
Keywords: Multi-layers filters KDF Chlorine Heavy metals Water treatment
A B S T R A C T
Multi-layers filters are usually used in conventional water treatment plants to purify water, reduce turbidity, and remove plankton and undesired odours. Multi-layers filter consists of basic layers represented in graded sand and supporting gravel layers. This research aims to study the efficiency of such filters after the addition of new layers made of copper and zinc particles by 50% per each with high purity, which are traditionally called KDF (Kinetic Degradation Fluxion). The purpose of adding such layers is to increase the efficiency of filters in the removal of diverse chemical contaminants of heavy elements (Lead, Nickel, iron, etc.), and chlorine to use them in tradi-tional or advanced filter stations, such reverse osmosis plants or ion exchange water filters. A glass tube was filled with KDF, sand and gravel layers and used as a multi-layer's filter, and tests were carried out on a water sample that has been prepared in the lab with concentration of 2mg/l for heavy elements and 5mg/l for chlorine. Flow rates of the tested water sample were 20, 40, 60, 80 and 100 l/hr. The results show a very high efficiency of such filters to remove of chlorine from the treated water by 90% and more, while the efficiency of the modified filter to remove lead, nickel and iron was 92%, 88%, and 67% respectively; however, there was no significant records in removing the biological contaminants.
1. Introduction
Multilayer filters are used in conventional water treatment plants to treat the small suspended particles and reduce the turbidity for drinking water or industrial purposes. Most of the disinfection processes are carried out on treated water when it used for drink, and they include physical and chemical methods to kill Bacteria and other pathogenic microbiology, which cause many diseases to human. Such processes are associated with side effect products called as (Disinfection By-products) as a result of reaction of chlorine with organic materials which exist in water sources and one of these materials is Tri-Halo Methane's THMs which considered as carcinogenic material (Tian et al., 2017). Chlorine in all its kinds is considered the main chemical disinfectant, which have been used in sterilizing water in 1908, and since that date chlorine was used as an effective sterilizer which helped in limiting the spread of diseases and epidemics that water was the main factor in their trans-mission. Though, it exceeded the amount of Penicillin and other anti-biotics in saving the lives of people (Laubusch E, 1971).
However, the use of chlorine showed side effects that resulted from the reaction of chlorine with the existing organic components in water Total Organics Carbons TOC (Hubrig and Innovation Services Inc,
2015). Researchers continued to provide disinfection methods and materials, which has less or no side effects to human and environment. And one of these methods is using new techniques and new materials to increase the efficiency of multi-layers’ filters' performance by reducing the side effects on hand and reducing costs on other hands (Xiony, 2004). Multi-layer filters differ in their components and design de-pending on the main purpose of using them in filtration plants. The most used material as filtering medium are sand which is used in special granular gradients and gravel as a supporting layer to the sand, which used to increase the efficiency of multiple filters. Other filtration mediums are added such as anthracite or activated carbon in its two types (granules and powder) to clear water from odours and undesir-able gases and reduce dissolved organic compounds to a minimum acceptable amount. A thin layer of activated carbon (sometimes fine carbon powder) could be added above the fine sand layer during the treatment process called absorptive treatment, in which gases and or-ganic materials are absorbed. Despite of the high efficiency of activated carbon in reducing and removing the soluble organic materials (Total Organic Carbon), the cost of re-activate and use it may not be useful especially when materials' concentration is more than 40% (Tobiason et al., 2011). A number of attempts were made to develop the performance of filters, and addition to the filtration process, some filters are designed to do other functions when chemical or biological mediums are added for the purposes of disin-fection and removal of organic compounds (Zeppenfeld, 2010). The researcher Labso noticed during his study, the effect of some metals on water properties when they are used to disinfect water. He found that when water passes on the surface of silver, the taste and odour of water are acceptably changed as a result of the effect of silver ion oxides on water properties and microbiology. That clarify the use of silver tubes or silver coated tubes to save water through old trips of commercial ship (Shuval et al., 1995). In 2004, NASA has introduced an innovative system for water purification and disinfection using electrolytic ioni-zation of silver used in the Apollo spacecraft (Sanchez et al., 2018). The performance of metals or chemical active compounds was not limited by sterilizing only, but also the mineral compounds were found to have the ability on reducing contaminants in water such as heavy elements and other dissolved ions. They can achieve such goal by mixing certain ratios of minerals' grains (with a high degree of purity) to form an alloy that causes an ion exchange reaction (oxidation and reduction reac-tions) in aqueous solutions, in addition to the ability of such metal combination on oxidizing micro-organisms. The reason of this adding had been confirmed by researcher Wangman by using a combination of Copper and Zinc powder (with a high degree of purity). He explained that free energy of the surface has a significant effect on the movement and transmission of ions in aqueous solutions, where these solutions provide electrolyte medium that helps activating the ions exchanging process. This technique has been used in reducing sediments in stream boilers, and the combination of such mineral compounds was laterally renamed as kinetic degradation fluxion (KDF) (Barakat, 2011). The Environment Protection Agency EPA has considered KDF as one of the materials, which are used in elimination of biogenic pollutants (algae, fungi and bacteria), in addition to its effectiveness in reducing heavy elements through oxidation and reduction processes. This material was also investigated to have the ability in removing mercury from water wells or other water sources by depending a specific combination of Zinc and Copper (AGENCY, 2009). Kinetic Degradation Fluxion (KDF) is a high-purity copper-zinc formulation that uses a basic chemical process known as redox (oxidation/reduction) to remove chlorine, lead, mercury, iron, and hydrogen sulfide from water supplies. The process also has a mild anti-bacterial, algaecidic, and fungicidic effect and may reduce the accumulation of lime scale (Spiegel et al., 2018). The KDF is environmentally friendly material; it can be washed by reverse washing way to reactivate and reuse again, and it has an operating life longer than other mediums in the multi-layers’ filters (Jeakle). The efficiency of such material is affected by the design factors of filters such as flow rate and surface area of the used material and its layer thickness, Also, the pH value has an impact on the efficiency and performance of this material (Tijing et al., 2010). Grains size for the combination of KDF material and the distribution method has a significant effect on its performance and efficiency and the success of the backwash during the process of reactivating multi-media filter, and it appeared through ex-periments that the addition of an appropriate layer of metal medium does not affect the pressure drop rates during the work of the multi-filter if used within standard operating flow rates for filtration (T. F. L. B, 2003).
The KDF redox process works by exchange electrons with con-taminants. This give and take of electrons converts may contaminants into harmless components during the reaction, electrons are transferred between molecules, and new elements are created. Some harmful contaminants are changed into harmless components. Free chlorine KDF filters are used most often in conjunction with an activated carbon filter. Similarly, some heavy metals such as copper, lead, mercury and others, react to plate out onto the medium's surface, thus being effec-tively removed from the water supply (Roy and Hydroviv, 2018).
This study aims to improve the quality of filters and reduce
environmental pollutants to protect human health and save the en-vironment, hence; a layer of a chemically active material called kinetic degradation fluxion (KDF) was added to the multi-layers’ filters. This layer is a combination of copper and zinc granules at a certain rate and high purity, and it helps increasing filters’ effectiveness in the elim-ination of soluble pollutants and heavy elements such as lead, nickel and iron. In addition, the study aims to determine the ability of this material in minimization of residual free chlorine concentration and some biological organisms in water.

2.1. Materials
KDF material supplied by a US company; which is composed of a mixture of high purity grains of copper and zinc, 50% per each. The volumetric gradient of the mixture grains ranges from 0.146 to 2.0mm. This material causes chemical reactions when it used in aqueous solu-tions such as Redox (Reduction and Oxidation), which are activated during the passing of water on this material, leading to a change in the properties of dissolved matters in the water. Oxidation of some material could happen while the reduction of other materials is in progress, thus dissolved pollutants are turned into insoluble sediments, which can be easily separated and disposed by reverse washing. In the case of chlorine, the reduction process turns it into undissolved chlorides, and with the same principle other dissolved elements gets oxidized and turned into insoluble elements in the water, such as lead, iron and nickel. 2.2. Sample collection and preparation
Contaminated water samples were prepared by adding 2mg of heavy elements (Lead, Iron and Nickel) in one litter of water. While chlorine samples were prepared with 5mg/l.
According to the standard method of water and wastewater treat-ment (American Public Health Association, 1998), the following de-vices and equipment were used in this research methodology:
a. Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometer: To measure concentrations of heavy metals (lead, iron, nickel) before and after treatment.
b. Turbidity meter: In order to measure the turbidity of treated water, a WTW device was used for this test.
c. PH measure: To measure the acidic function of treated water, a WTW device was used for this test

2.2.1. Method Several experiments were carried out on water after the addition of
specific contaminants. Some water samples were containing chlorine with a concentration 5mg/s, while others were prepared with 2mg/l of heavy elements (lead, iron and nickel). The flow rate was determined at a range of 20–80 L/hour, and water were flowing down by gravity to a sand filter from a tank placed at the top of the filter tube. KDF layer was added with a thickness 50mm and placed above a 140-mm sand layer which was supported by a 60mm layer of gravel at the bottom. After each experiment, the samples were withdrawn by the adoption of the aforementioned various flow rates, then the samples were examined by the atomic spectrometer to measure the concentrations of heavy ele-ments. While the free chlorine was measured by the chlorine test kite, besides other specifications of synthetic water, such as the pH, tur-bidity, and concentration of dissolved salts and water hardness, were also examined.
After each experiment the medium's layers were replaced to prevent the interacting of the previous experiments' contaminants with the new sampled water, and to ensure that they are not affecting the results of another metal removing tests. Then water was pumped into the filter with different operational flow rates (80, 60, 40, 20 l/hr); thereafter, chemical and physics tests were made after each experiment to study the effect of the new metal layer on performance of a filter towards contaminates reduction.

Hence, the ion exchanging rate between chlorine and the KDF layer is dropping at a short deten-tion time due to incomplete reaction. However, the slow flow rates are not always the optimal or most efficient in design since the process of reaction activation requires an appropriate amount of water solution to ensure the ion exchanging. It can be concluded from results that the highest rate of chlorine removal occurred when using a 40 L/hour flow rate.
Conversely, other water samples were prepared with a constant flow rate (40 L/hour) and results were taken at different time to determine the effect of the detention time on filter efficiency. The efficiency of filter is decreasing when increasing the detention time above 25min, and with increasing time to 65min the efficiency sig-nificantly decreased. 4. Conclusions
The results indicate that the KDF can be used as an additional layer to improve the performance of multi-layered filters, as it is useful to reduce design costs of processing units, particularly in the treatment of industrial water. It can be also used in reverse osmosis water treatment
plants since they have high efficiency in chlorine reduction. They are useful in ion exchanges for their efficiency in reducing and removing certain heavy elements and chlorine, also can reduce the cost of maintenance and increase the operational age of the ion exchanger. Additionally, the use of KDF layer in traditional filters helps to reduce the cost and extend the operational life of the filters’media especially to the activated carbon layer, and is an environmentally friendly material.
